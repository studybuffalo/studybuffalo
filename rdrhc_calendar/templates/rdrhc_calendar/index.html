Using These Calendars

The calendar files are saved as an .ics file. This file type is a standard form in which numerous calendar and scheduling programs can use to share the same calendar. These files are hosted here so that you can keep your calendar synced up across multiple locations and programs, or to share a schedule with someone else.

The instructions for how to utilize these files will vary depending on the program. Below are several guides on how to set up some of the common calendar programs. Some calendars support adding either an online calendar (one hosted on a website) or an offline calendars (one you download to your computer and manually add). We recommend following the online instructions, as this will ensure your calendar automatically stays up-to-date when changes are made.

  • Smart Phone - Online Calendar
  • Apple iPhone Calendars - Online Calendar
  • Microsoft Outlook (2013) - Online Calendar
  • Microsoft Outlook (2013) - Offline Calendar
  • Google Calendar - Online Calendar
  • Google Calendar - Offline Calendar

Smart Phone - Online Calendar

  1. View the email received from calendar@studybuffalo.com regarding your schedule from your smart phone.
  2. Click on the link to your schedule located at the bottom of the email.
  3. Your device should automatically subscribe to your work schedule calendar. If this does not work, please proceed to the Manual Set-Up instructions below.

iPhone Calendars - Online Calendar

  1. Start by going into your iPhone settings by clicking the Settings icon.
  2. Scroll down in the Settings menu and click on the Calendar item.
  3. Click on the Accounts item.
  4. Click on the Add Account item.
  5. Click on the Other item.
  6. Click on the Add Subscribed Calendar item.
  7. Enter in the web address to your calendar (e.g. "http://www.studybuffalo.com/calendar/example.ics").
  8. Click the Next button. If you get an error here, make sure you have entered in your calendar correctly.
  9. If desired, you can specify a title for the calendar by changing the Description field. Make sure you leave the User Name and Password fields blank and the Use SSL button off. Click the Save button to add the calendar. Your calendar should now be accessible in your iPhone calendar apps.

Microsoft Outlook (2013) - Online Calendar

  1. Open up Microsoft Outlook and click on the Calendar tab.
  2. Click on the Open Calendar button.
  3. From the drop-down menu, select the From Internet... button.
  4. In the New Internet Calendar Subscription pop-up, enter in your calendar web address. NOTE: You will need to replace the "http" in the web address with "webcal". For example, http://www.studybuffalo.com/calendar/example.ics would become webcal://www.studybuffalo.com/calendar/example.ics.
  5. Click the OK button. If you don't get the pop-up in step 6, double check that you entered the proper address in step 4 (including the "webcal" portion).
  6. You should receive another pop-up asking if you want to add this calendar and subscribe to updates. Click Yes to finishing adding the calendar.

Microsoft Outlook (2013) - Offline Calendar

  1. Open up Microsoft Outlook and click the File button.
  2. Click on the Open & Export item.
  3. Click on the Open Calendar item.
  4. Find the .ics calendar file, select it, and click on the OK button. Your calendar should now be added to your Microsoft Outlook account.

Google Calendar - Online Calendar

  1. Open up your Google Calendar ( https://www.calendar.google.com).
  2. Click on the triangle menu button under the Other calendars section.
  3. Click on the Add by URL item.
  4. Add in the web address to the calendar you want to add.
  5. Click on the Add Calendar button to add the calendar to you Google Calendar account.
  6. If you want to customize the settings to your newly added calendar, click on the triangle menu button of the calendar and select Calendar settings.
  7. Change the settings you want, such as the Calendar Name.
  8. When you are finished changing your settings, click the Save button.

Google Calendar - Offline Calendar

  1. Open up your Google Calendar ( https://www.calendar.google.com).
  2. Click on the gear icon.
  3. Click on the Settings item.
  4. Click on the Calendars link.
  5. Click on the Import calendar link.
  6. Click on the Choose File button.
  7. Find the .ics calendar file, select it, and click on the Open button.
  8. Choose which Calendar you want to add the new calendar events to and click the Import button. It is convenient to create a new calendar specifically for the work schedule, as it is easier to upload a new version of the .ics file at a later date. Continue on to step 9 to see how to create a new calendar.
  9. Complete steps 1-4 to get to the Calendar Settings Page.
  10. Click on the Create new calendar button.
  11. Enter in a Calendar Name. You may also fill in the other fields if desired.
  12. Click the Create Calendar button. You may complete steps 1-8 again and import into this calendar if desired.

Program Information & Disclaimers

Security

To make your schedule accessible to all your devices and programs, the calendar must be: 1) publically available; and 2) hosted on our own personal server.

  1. This means that anyone with your schedule link could view your calendar. To minimize the risk of this, the calendar file does not actually use your name, but rather a code name. These names are generally a long enough/complex enough that it makes someone guessing your calendar very unlikely.
  2. While our own servers are also secured, we would suspect the safety of the AHS Network would still be superior. To our knowledge all major steps have been taken to secure the server, but this can change rapidly and it can take time to implement fixes.

Disclaimers

This program relies on a number of assumptions to properly generate a correct work schedule. There are numerous factors that can cause issues with the generation of these schedules, such as new shift codes, typos in the Microsoft Excel document, or duplicate names on the spreadsheet. While we do try to account for many of these, it will never be perfect. To reduce these risks, you will be emailed whenever your updated schedule varies from the previous version of the schedule. You will also receive a list of all the assumptions the program made. You are strongly encouraged to review these emails for any possible errors.

There is also the risk that there will be an issue with our own servers that may cause delays in schedule updates. While we do try to limit these occurrences, there is always the possibility of unexpected issues that can take several days (or longer) to resolve.

Finally, this whole project is done on our own spare time with our own resources. We am happy to provide some degree of assistance in using these programs (largely in the forms of tutorials or how-to documents), but from a practical standpoint we cannot provide individual and personalized tech support.

Customizability

We do offer some degree of customization to you schedule. Due to the potential volume of requests from multiple users, the options are limited to the following:

  • Choosing your anonymous calendar name (pending availability of names)
  • Have calendar generate shifts as "Full Day" events
  • Customize start and stop times for specific shifts

Should you wish to make use of any of these customizations, please let us know and we will update it at my earliest convenience.
