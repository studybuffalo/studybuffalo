Tools > QTc Risk Tool

Welcome to the QTc Risk Tool. This tool helps assess a patient's risk of QTc-prolongation and Torsades de Pointes

  • One QTc-prolonging medication
  • Two or more QTc-prolonging medication
  • Serum K+ ≤ 3.6 mmol/L
  • Serum Ca2+ ≤ 1.16 mmol/L
  • Serum Mg2+ ≤ 0.7 mmol/L
  • Age ≥ 68
  • Female sex
  • Loop diuretic
  • Admission QTc ≥ 450 ms
  • Recent MI (in past 7 days)
  • Sepsis
  • Heart failure
  • Anorexia nervosa or starvation
  • Bradycardia (heart rate < 45 beats/min)
  • Diabetes mellitus
  • Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy
  • Hypoglycemia (documented and in the absence of diabetes)
  • Intoxication with QT-prolonging drug (<= 24 h)
  • Long QT syndrome
  • Pheochromocytoma
  • Renal dialysis
  • Status after AF conversion (7 days after cardioversion, radio-frequency ablation, or the Maze procedure)
  • Status after cardiac arrest (past 24 h)
  • Status after syncope or seizure (past 24 h)
  • Stroke, SAH, head trauma (past 7 days)
