Disclaimers & Notices

This calculator is based off the prices listed off the Alberta Blue Cross Interactive Drug Benefit List (iDBL). While we strive to keep our information updated and accurate, there may be issues outside of our control. The data found on the iDBL will supersede anything reported here. We are not responsible for how this information is used - the following are considerations to make when using this website:

  • To reduce the burden on the Alberta Blue Cross servers, we update this information weekly (last updated on ).
  • Much of this information is automatically generated - this could result in inaccuracies in the data that we have not caught.
  • Prices are calculated assuming that the pharmacy is charging the maximum amount allowed in the Alberta Blue Cross Pharmacy Services Agreement. Pharmacies may choose to charge less than these amounts when billing a third-party coverage. Pharmacies may choose to charge more than these amounts for patients paying out-of-pocket.
  • The benefit information listed is only accurate for publicly-funded Alberta Blue Cross insurance plans. While it may be applicable to other insurance plans, we cannot guarantee this. Other insurance plans may choose to cover different products at different prices.
  • The prices generated when applying third party coverage cannot be guaranteed. We calculate these prices based on the information provided publically by Alberta Blue Cross, but this information may have changed since we last updated and there may be additional client costs/fees that we are unaware of (e.g. deductibles, plan maximums).

If you have any questions, concerns, or wish to report an error, please email us at studybuffalo@studybuffalo.com.

Price Calculations

Below is an example of how prices are calculated, with the example of 100 tablets of a medication (Unit Price = $0.10/tablet):

Medication Cost

The cost per unit multiplied by the number of units. \begin{align} Medication\ Cost & = Cost\ Per\ Unit * Quantity &\\ & = \frac{$0.10}{tablet} * 100\ tablets &\\ & = $10.00 \end{align}

Allowable Upcharge #1

3% of the medication cost. \begin{align} Allowable\ Upcharge\ \#1 & = Medication\ Cost * 0.03 &\\ & = $10.00 * 0.03 &\\ & = $0.30 \end{align}

Allowable Upcharge #2

6% of the total of medication cost & allowable upcharge #1. \begin{align} Allowable\ Upcharge\ \#2 & = \left(Medication\ Cost + Allowable\ Upcharge\ \#1\right) * 0.06 &\\ & = \left($10.00 + $0.30\right) * 0.06 &\\ & = $10.30 * 0.06 &\\ & = $0.62 \end{align}

Dispensing Fee

A flat fee of $12.30.

Final Price

All the above fees are added to the medication cost to generate the final total. \begin{align} Total\ Cost & = Medication\ Cost + Allowable\ Upcharge\ \#1 + Allowable\ Upcharge\ \#2 + Dispensing\ Fee &\\ & = $10.00 + $0.30 + $0.62 + $12.30 &\\ & = $23.22 \end{align}

Third Party Coverage Calculations

Third Party Coverage Coverage Details
Non-Group Coverage (Group 1)
  • Patient pays 30% of the final drug cost.
  • The patient will only pay a maximum of $25.00 per prescription refill.
  • Plans may be subject to annual deductibles and annual maximums (e.g. $600 maximum on diabetes supplies).
  • If patient chooses a brand of medication with a price exceeding the least-cost alternative, the third party provider will only pay what they would pay for that LCA. The patient will be required to pay the remaining difference.
